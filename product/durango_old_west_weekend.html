Durango & Silverton Narrow Gauge Railroad Train

Today, we ride the Durango & Silverton Narrow Gauge Railroad, known worldwide as one of America's finest railroads. Ride a historic coal-fired steam locomotive that has been in operation since 1882 and relive last year's sights and sounds in a spectacular ride. Experience a breathtaking canyon train in the secluded wilderness of the San Juan National Forest, a Colorado wilderness like former miners and pioneers. After breakfast, board the bus and drive along the Million Dollar Highway to Silverton, Colorado. With breathtaking views of mountains, valleys and canyons, the Million Dollar Highway winds and hugs the mountains, offering dramatic views everywhere. The moment you get off the bus and meet a local guide, Silverton's roots have been dismantled, making you feel like you're back in time. Our guide will introduce you to many of the fascinating sights of the historic district, including Blair Street, which is known by many women. Then drive to Cunningham Gulch and Old Hand Red Gold Mine. Enjoy the scenery, fresh air and freshly made picnic lunch in the picnic area before tasting gold and silver bread! To travel 1/3 mile 1,600 feet to the center of Mount Galina, wear a suit and board an electric mining train. Here you can see gold veins with tunnels and shafts built by hard rock miners between 1907 and 1972. After returning to the sun, continue your tour overlooking the guesthouse stubbornly mounted on a steep slope. On the way back to town, a guide will pass through the Mayflower Mill, explaining how the ore was processed in the late 19th century. Next destinations are the state-of-the-art mining museum and the historic Silverton Prison. Once in jail, you'll feel like you're "behind the bar" in Old Town Silverton. Durango and Silverton narrow-gauge steam locomotives arrive at Silverton Station in time for Durango. On your way to the smelter, ride the same rails that transported gold and silver ore and enjoy the breathtaking views of the same Animas River Canyon that train crews enjoyed in the 1880s. .. Tonight I will return to the station for dinner at T's Smokehouse & Grill. T's specializes in smoked hickory barbecue, Southern and Cajun dishes, freshly ground Angus burgers, and Leidenheimer bread po'boys shipped from New Orleans..

Mesa Verde National Park

Mesa Verde National Park is about 35 miles from Durango. Today, spend the day exploring the national park, founded in 1906. The National Park was established from 600 to 1300 AD to preserve and interpret the archaeological heritage of Pueblo's ancestors, which was built in his hometown for over 700 years. Today, the park protects nearly 5,000 known archaeological sites, including 600 cave dwellings. Sometime in the late 1190s, after living primarily in Mesapeake for 600 years, many ancestral Pueblos began to live in Pueblo, which was built under an overhanging cliff. Structures ranged in size from one-room storage unites to villages with more than 150 rooms. The first stop is the Mesa Verde National Park Visitor and Research Center, where you can learn about the park and see its native crafts. Away from the center, there are archaeological sites along Mesaloop Road. A 6-mile drive offers 12 surface dwellings and cliff-top views such as Sunpoint Overlook, Square Tower House, and spectacular views of Cliff Palace. Our guide points out some of the most notable and best-preserved cliff dwelling ruins. Visit the famous Cliffs Palace, the largest and most famous cliff dwelling, with more than 150 private rooms and more than 20 Kiva (religious ceremonial rooms). Enter a very large apartment that may have had a special meaning to the original resident. The Mesa Verde Cliff Palace is believed to have been a social and administrative place often used for ceremonies. Tonight we'll have dinner at a local brewing pub and head to Durango's most popular and largest adult playground, the Wild West Saloon.

Whitewater Rafting or Jeep Tour / Afternoon At Leisure

This morning you can choose a torrent or a jeep tour. Both last for about 4 hours. Experience the adrenaline-inspiring excitement of a rafting adventure while cruising along the clear waters of the historic Animas River. Paddle class I, II, and intermittent Class III torrents with an experienced river guide and enjoy views of Durango from the water and natural beauty to the south of the city. Enjoy an off-road expedition to La Plata Canyon (20 minutes west of Durango), a hidden treasure surrounded by the La Plata Mountains. In the spring, several small waterfalls and running rivers follow steep jeep trails, along with wildlife such as marmots, mule deer, herds of elks, jankos, kingfishers, turkey vultures, red-tailed hawks, and bald eagles. Our knowledgeable guides entertain us with stories from the old mining era and interpret local geography, flora and fauna when traveling to the top of the Kennebec Pass. When you reach the summit, disembark and stretch your legs to take a picture of Durango's most famous summit. This afternoon, you are free to shop and explore Durango yourself or with your new friends. Durango is the perfect place to shop for over 500 stores, from clothing stores, household goods stores and gift shops to bicycle and outdoor goods stores. Need to do something more active? Then go hiking, try ziplines, kayak or rent a bike. No matter what you decide, you can have a great afternoon. Tonight, celebrate a great time at Diamond Belle Saloon, one of the most famous and original ragtime piano bars of the Archetypal Old West. The bell menu includes some of the local favorites offered by the famous Bell Girls in period costumes. Listen to good music and enjoy fine dining at the Fairwell Dinner.
